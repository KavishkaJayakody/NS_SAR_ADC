8-Bit NS-SAR ADC Core

Capacitor Array Sizing Analysis

For a well-designed SAR ADC, quantization noise should typically dominate over other noise sources, of which thermal noise (\(kT/C\)) is the most significant. To maximize the performance of the ADC and preserve the Effective Number of Bits (ENOB), we must ensure that \( kT/C \ll P_q \) (Quantization Noise Power).

Note: For these derivations and calculations, a single/differential-ended ADC models are used as required.

1. Thermal Noise Model

The noise power introduced across the DAC capacitor by the sampling resistor can be modeled as:

\[ P_{tn} = 4kTR \cdot B \]

Where \( R \) is the total noise-generating resistance, and \( B \) is the noise equivalent bandwidth. Due to the RC low-pass filter nature of the sampling network, the noise equivalent bandwidth is:

\[ B = \frac{1}{2\pi RC} \]

While a rough estimate using the bandwidth yields \( \frac{2kT}{\pi C} \), integrating the noise power across the RC network over the entire frequency range yields the exact total thermal noise power:

\[ P_{tn} = \frac{kT}{C} \]

If we set an upper limit to \( kT/C \), then the total sampling thermal noise will be bounded. Hereafter, we will refer to this thermal noise power as \( P_{kt/c} = kT/C \).

2. SNR and ENOB Degradation

For an ideal quantizer, the Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) in dB is governed by:

\[ \text{SNR}_{ideal}\text{(dB)} \approx 6.02 \cdot N + 1.76 \]

(where \( N \) is the number of bits)

When other noise sources are considered, the overall SNR decreases, resulting in a lower ENOB:

\[ \text{SNR(dB)} = 10 \log_{10}\left(\frac{P_{sig}}{P_{tot}}\right) \]

5. Capacitor Array Mismatch Analysis (Pelgrom's Model)

To avoid linearity degradation (INL/DNL), matching between adjacent unit capacitors (\( C_u \)) must strictly adhere to Pelgrom's mismatch model:

\[ \sigma\left(\frac{\Delta C}{C}\right) = \frac{A_c}{\sqrt{\text{Area}}} \]

5.1 SPICE Model Extraction for Sky130 MiM Capacitors

We target the Metal-Insulator-Metal (MiM) capacitor devices available in the sky130_fd_pr library (sky130_fd_pr__cap_mim_m3_1 / _2). The PDK SPICE model defines matching distributions as follows:

.param carea = 'camimc*(wc)*(lc)' .param cperim = 'cpmimc*((wc)+(lc))*2' .param czero = 'carea + cperim' dev/gauss='0.01*2.8*(carea + cperim)/sqrt(wc*lc*mf)'

Extracting the Gaussian deviation parameter yields the Pelgrom area coefficient: \( A_c = 0.01 \cdot 2.8 = 0.028 \) (or \( 2.8\% \cdot \mu\text{m} \)).

5.2 Mathematical Grounding: Stochastic Analysis of the DAC

The output of a capacitive DAC is defined by the ratio of the switched capacitors to the total capacitance. In a fully differential architecture, the differential output \(V_{diff}\) is:

\[ V_{diff} = (V_{P+} - V_{P-}) - (V_{N+} - V_{N-}) \]

The Unit Capacitor Model
Each unit capacitor \( C_i \) is modeled as an independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) random variable: \( C_i \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_{C}, \sigma_{C}^2) \). The relative standard deviation (coefficient of variation) is defined by Pelgrom’s Law:

\[ \frac{\sigma_{C}}{\mu_{C}} = \frac{A_C}{\sqrt{W \cdot L}} \]

Variance of a Binary Weighted Bit
The \(k\)-th bit capacitor \(C_k\) is the sum of \(n = 2^{k-1}\) unit capacitors. By the property of the sum of independent normal variables:

\[ Var(C_k) = \sum_{i=1}^{2^{k-1}} Var(C_u) = 2^{k-1} \cdot \sigma_u^2 \]

The Differential DNL at Mid-Scale
The Differential Non-Linearity (DNL) is worst at the MSB transition (\(0... \to 1...\)). The error \(E_{DNL}\) is the difference between the MSB capacitor and the sum of all lower-order capacitors (\(C_{rem}\)):

\[ E_{DNL} = C_{MSB} - C_{rem} \]

For an 8-bit ADC, \(C_{MSB}\) has \(2^7 = 128\) units, and \(C_{rem}\) also has 128 units. In a single-ended setup:

\[ Var(E_{DNL, single}) = Var(C_{128}) + Var(C_{128}) = 256 \cdot \sigma_u^2 \]

In a fully differential system, we have two such arrays (\(P\) and \(N\)) acting in opposition. The total differential error variance is:

\[ Var(E_{DNL, diff}) = Var(E_{DNL, P}) + Var(E_{DNL, N}) = 256\sigma_u^2 + 256\sigma_u^2 = 512\sigma_u^2 \] \[ \sigma_{DNL, diff} = \sqrt{512} \cdot \sigma_u = 2^{(N+1)/2} \cdot \sigma_u \]

5.3 Derivation of the Sizing Constraint

To ensure DNL < 0.5 LSB with a 3σ yield (99.7%), we set the boundary condition: \( 3 \cdot \sigma_{DNL, diff} < 0.5 \cdot \mu_C \). Substituting the differential variance:

\[ 3 \cdot \sqrt{512} \cdot \sigma_u < 0.5 \cdot \mu_C \] \[ 67.88 \cdot \sigma_u < 0.5 \cdot \mu_C \implies \frac{\sigma_u}{\mu_C} < \frac{0.5}{67.88} \approx 0.007366 \]

5.4 Physical Sizing for SKY130

Given the PDK parameters: \(A_C = 0.028 \text{ }\mu\text{m}\), \(C_{area} = 2 \text{ fF/}\mu\text{m}^2\), and \(C_{perim} = 0.19 \text{ fF/}\mu\text{m}\).

Step 1: Solving for Minimum Area
Using Pelgrom's Equation:

\[ \sqrt{\text{Area}} > \frac{A_C}{0.007366} = \frac{0.028}{0.007366} \approx 3.801 \text{ }\mu\text{m} \implies \text{Area}_{min} \approx 14.45 \text{ }\mu\text{m}^2 \]

Step 2: Determining Dimensions (Square Geometry)
For a square capacitor to minimize fringe field asymmetry:

\[ W = L = \sqrt{14.45} \approx \mathbf{3.8 \text{ }\mu\text{m}} \]

Step 3: Final Unit Capacitance (\(C_u\)) Calculation

\[ C_{total} = (14.45 \cdot 2.0) + (4 \cdot 3.8 \cdot 0.19) = 28.9 + 2.888 = \mathbf{31.79 \text{ fF}} \]

5.6 Practical Unit Capacitor Selection

The calculations in the previous section establish the absolute lower limit for the unit capacitor (\( W = L = 3.8 \text{ }\mu\text{m} \), \( C_u \approx 31.8 \text{ fF} \)) to satisfy stochastic matching constraints. For layout simplicity and to provide an additional margin for matching, we evaluate slightly larger, integer-dimensioned geometries:

  • Option A: \( 4 \text{ }\mu\text{m} \times 4 \text{ }\mu\text{m} \)
    • Area = \( 16 \text{ }\mu\text{m}^2 \), Perimeter = \( 16 \text{ }\mu\text{m} \)
    • \( C_{u} = (16 \cdot 2.0) + (16 \cdot 0.19) = 32.0 + 3.04 = \mathbf{35.04 \text{ fF}} \)
  • Option B: \( 5 \text{ }\mu\text{m} \times 5 \text{ }\mu\text{m} \)
    • Area = \( 25 \text{ }\mu\text{m}^2 \), Perimeter = \( 20 \text{ }\mu\text{m} \)
    • \( C_{u} = (25 \cdot 2.0) + (20 \cdot 0.19) = 50.0 + 3.8 = \mathbf{53.8 \text{ fF}} \)

We will choose one of these two practical unit sizes for the final layout implementation based on the final routing density and available area.

6. Architecture Implications and Area Feasibility

A standard binary-weighted Capacitor DAC for an 8-bit differential architecture requires 512 total unit capacitors (256 per side).

\[ \text{Total Active Cap Area} = 512 \cdot 14.45 \text{ }\mu\text{m}^2 \approx 7,398.4 \text{ }\mu\text{m}^2 \]
  • Required Area (Array Only): ~7,400 μm²
  • Available Area (Total 1x2 Tiles): 36,000 μm² (160 x 225 μm)

Unlike previous worst-case approximations, this stochastic analysis proves that implementing a standard full-binary CDAC is highly feasible within the allocated TinyTapeout area constraints without requiring complex area-reduction strategies.
